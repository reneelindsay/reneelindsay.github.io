You make your coffee slowly and with great care. No one can prepare coffee the way you can. In fact, you cause a gigantic fus when someone offers you coffee brewed by any other method than the one you have perfected over thousands of devoted mornings. Well-intentioned half-wits do not hold to your most excellent standard of taste and refinement.

  1. Grind whole beans from Caffe Ibis, a local company.
  2. Bring a small pot of water to a simmer, not a boil.
  3. Steep for exactly four minutes in a French press.
  4. Carefully pour into your favorite, handmade mug.
  5. Add two and three quarters tablespoons of organic, grass-fed half and half.
  6. Allow to cool to one hundred and fifty-seven-point-eight degrees Farenheit.
  7. Sip.
  8. You are refined.

Brewing coffee with a French press is only one of many ways to prepare a cuppa joe. There's the Moka pot, which produces a rich, potent java akin to espresso. There's also the syphon method for mad scientists who want an immersive experience. Don't use those little pod thingys. Seriously. Save the planet.
